Good Continuation and Closure

“The eye tends to build a relationship between elements, it fills in the gaps, and identifies hidden motion in the design.” [13] Wertheimer suggested that good continuation could be seen when a segment proceeds in a direction that “carr[ies] on the principle logically demanded” from the original element [5]. The eyes will follow a shape, even beyond its end point, until its logical conclusion.

Closure is the ability of the human mind to fill in gaps. We try to close open figures to perceive it as a whole object, rather than separate features. This can be seen when Graphical User Interfaces (GUIs) represent collections of objects as stacks, with one whole object showing and the edges of others “behind” it [4].
