Gestalt Psychology and User Centered Design

In his book The Design of Everyday Things, Don Norman says “good designs fit our needs so well that the design is invisible.” [1] Author Steve Krug argues that good design should lighten the user’s cognitive load in Don’t Make Me Think [2]. User Interaction and User Experience (UI/UX) Design uses the principles of human perception to create designs that are intuitive to the point of being invisible. These principles were first proposed over one hundred years ago by Gestalt Psychologist Max Wertheimer. The world of technology may have changed significantly since then, but the basics of human perception remain the same.

Join me in exploring Gestalt Psychology and its use in UX/UI Design.
