Psychology and Human Perception

In the early twentieth century, psychologist Max Wertheimer theorized that the mind and body could not be separated when observing how people perceive their surroundings. This led to the field of Gestalt psychology and the foundation for modern views of perception: “Gestalt theory emphasized that the whole of anything is greater than its parts [3].” We view our surroundings not piecemeal but in terms of whole objects [4]. Gestalt psychologists identified what are now known as the classic principles of perceptual grouping [5].

Modern perceptual and cognitive psychologists have refined these classic ideas to create new principles of grouping, quantified by measures of phenomenological psychophysics [6].

  • Common region
  • Synchrony
  • Element connectedness
  • Induced grouping

Together, these principles create a framework for effective UI/UX design.
