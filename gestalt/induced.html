Induced Grouping

Grouping can be induced by placing elements with no basis for grouping in proximity to those with their own grouping relationships [5]. Conversely, induced grouping can be disrupted by “using common region to put the inducing set into a separate region of space” [5]. This can be the most powerful grouping principle and should be used carefully; there are ethical considerations to induced grouping. Contextual advertising uses the principles of induced grouping to create ads that look like posts from friends in Facebook or Twitter feeds [10]. This can be very effective, but also invites criticism.
